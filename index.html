Overview

This website provides an overview of a project done by Team Vulpini from the 2021 class of the Master's in Educational Technology and Applied Learning Sciences (METALS) program in the School of Computer Science at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU). METALS culminates with a capstone project that is the focus of the second and third semesters of the program - in this seven-month project for an external client, students apply the techniques, theories, and methodologies that they have mastered in the program to deliver a final prototype for their client. Students gain practical experience while they work with faculty and industry mentors in a team-based research and development project. The overview of the entire project can be covered by our hunt statement where we identify our project goal and scope in the timeline.

Below you will find some additional details of the project, including the project timeline and the hunt statement we developed with our client, Schoolified, an early-stage start-up (at the time) that is developing classroom technology to improve remote learning situations for students, teachers, and parents.
